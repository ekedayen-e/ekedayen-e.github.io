The Timeline Of Mandela's Life

Nelson Mandela was a renowned apartheid opposer and political figure who fought for the equality of white and black people living in South Africa during a vicious segregation era.

  • 1918 - Born In Mvezo, South Africa.

  • 1943 - Graduated from the University Of South Africa.

  • 1944 - Joined the African National Congress.

  • 1952 - Became the National Volunteer in Chief of the Defiance Campaign.

  • 1956 - Arrested for Treason.

  • 1961 - Led strike against the government.

  • 1964 - Arrested again and sent to Robben Island.

  • 1988 - He was diagnosed with tuberculosis.

  • 1990 - He was released from prison.

  • 1991 - He was elected ANC president which ended white supremacy over the minority.

  • 1993 - Won Nobel Peace Prize

  • 1994 - Voted for the first time and was elected President

  • 1999 - Created the Nelson Mandela Foundation and Mandela Rhodes Foundation.

  • 2013 - He died in his home at Johannesburg.

    • “I have fought against white domination, and I have fought against black domination. I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve. But if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die. ” - Nelson Mandela
